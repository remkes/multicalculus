If a \(\sigma\) is very small, we have a very precise observable. If \(\sigma\) is large, the possible values in a reasonable probabity are much larger. The product of these two \(\sigma\) is the product of the uncertain in the measurement of position and moment. This is the famous uncertainty princple.
