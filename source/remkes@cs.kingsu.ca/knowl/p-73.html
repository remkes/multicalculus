Finally, a plane can be determined by three points (as long as the three points do not all lie on the same line). If I am given three points (\(p\text{,}\) \(q\) and \(r\)), then I can use \(p\) as the local origin and construct the local direction vectors as \(q-p\) and \(r-p\text{.}\) Then I can proceed as in the previous step: the normal is the cross product of the two local direction, \((q-p) \times (r-p)\text{.}\) Finally, I finish with the first step, using the normal and a point to find the constant \(d\text{.}\) Note, with this data, I have three points to chose from for this final step.
