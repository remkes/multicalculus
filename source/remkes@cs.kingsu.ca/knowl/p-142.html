The arclength function measures the length of the curve as a function of the parameter; it is simply the distance travelled along the curve. For example, if \(t \in [0,10]\text{,}\) then \(s(3)\) is the distance along the curve from \(\gamma(0)\) to \(\gamma(3)\text{,}\) \(s(5)\) is the distance the curve has covered from \(\gamma(0)\) to \(\gamma(5)\text{,}\) \(s(8)\) is the distance along the curve from \(\gamma(0)\) to \(\gamma(8)\) and so on. Since \(s\) depends on \(t\) outside the integral, I have to choose a temporary variable \(u\) inside the integral; in the integral, \(t\) is simply repalced with \(u\text{.}\)
