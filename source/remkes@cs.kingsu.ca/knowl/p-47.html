The use of cross products in rotational dynamics is ubiquitous. In fluid dynamics, local rotational motion of the fluid result in turbulence, vortices and similar effects. Tornadoes and hurricanes are particularly extreme examples of vortices. All the descriptions of the force and motion of these vortices involve cross products in the vectors describing the fluid.
