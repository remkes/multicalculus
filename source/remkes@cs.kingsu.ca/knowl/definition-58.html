Definition 6.3.2.

An closed interval \(I\) in \(\RR^n\) is the (geometric) product of single-variable closed intervals.
\begin{equation*} I = [a_1,b_1] \times [a_2,b_2] \times \ldots \times [a_n,b_n] \end{equation*}
This (geometric) product is defined to be the subset of \(\RR^n\) consisting of all \((x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)\) such that \(a_1 \leq x_1 \leq b_1\text{,}\) \(a_2 \leq x_2, \leq b_2\) and so on until \(a_n \leq x_n \leq b_n\text{.}\) In \(\RR^2\) it is a rectangle. In \(\RR^3\) it is a rectangular prism. Similarly, by using open interval, \((a,b)\) in the definition, I can define an open interval in \(\RR^n\text{.}\) Likewise, I can define various types of partially closed, partially open intervals by changing the inequalities (strict or non-strict) of the various endpoints of the single variable intervals in the product.
