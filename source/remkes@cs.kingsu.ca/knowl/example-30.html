Example 3.1.10.

Consider \(f(x,y) = \frac{5}{x^2+y^2+1}\text{.}\) It’s graph is Figure 3.1.8. This function has a simple hill at the origin and slopes down in all directions. The contours are loci of the form \(\frac{5}{x^2 + y^2 + 1} = c\text{,}\) which can be rearranged as \(\frac{5}{c} - 1 = x^2 + y^2\text{.}\) These contours are all circles, and are shown in Figure 3.1.11.
