A major goal of Calculus III was the extention of the derivative to multivariable functions: particularly to parametric curves and scalar fields. This course pursues the matching goal of extending the integral to multivariable functions. The course will consider new kinds of integrals involving parametric curves in Section 10.2, as part of the material called Vector Calculus. For the start, though, I’m interesting in scalar fields. What should it mean to integrate a scalar field? Well, there are two kinds of integral: definite and indefinite, so the questions needs to asked twice. First, I’m going to think about indefinite integrals.
