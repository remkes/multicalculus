In a parametric curve, everything changes in time. I can adjust this approximation by considering that \(x\) and \(y\) change over time. If \(\Delta t\) is the change in time over one of these segments, then by multiplying and dividing by \(\Delta t\text{,}\) and taking the division into the square root, I get the following expression.
\begin{equation*} L \cong \sum \sqrt{ \frac{\Delta x^2}{\Delta t^2} + \frac{\Delta y^2}{\Delta t^2}} \Delta t \end{equation*}
