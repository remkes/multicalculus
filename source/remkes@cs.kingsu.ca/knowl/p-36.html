This can be used to calculate the angle between two vectors simply by calculating the inverse cosine.
\begin{equation*} \theta = \arccos \left( \frac{u \cdot v}{|u||v|} \right) \end{equation*}
The output of the inverse cosine is an angle in the range \([0, \pi) \text{.}\) This convention works well for vectors, since the angle between them can always be chosen to be in this range. A useful implication of this relationship between angles and dot-products is that dot products can be used to identify perpendicular vectors.
