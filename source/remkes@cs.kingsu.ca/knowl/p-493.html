Since scalar fields are defined in several dimensions, the collection of maxima/minima can be complicated sets. The function \(f(x,y) = \cos (x+y)\text{,}\) shown in Figure FigureÂ 5.1.6 has a maximum whenever \(x+y\) is an even multiple of \(\pi\text{.}\) Each of those sets is a whole line, \(x+y=0\text{,}\) \(x+y = 2\pi\) and so on. For functions of two variables, its easy to have lines or curves of maximum or minimum points. In higher dimensions, there can be surfaces or hypersurfaces of maximum or minimum points.
