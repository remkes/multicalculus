As with \(\RR^2\text{,}\) the point \((a,b,c) \in \RR^3\) is the unique point I find by moving \(a\) units in the \(x\) direction, \(b\) units in the \(y\) direction and \(c\) units in the \(z\) direction. When visualizing \(\RR^2\text{,}\) the convention is to draw the \(x\) axis horizontally, with a positive direction to the right, and the \(y\) axis vertically, with a positive direction upwards.
