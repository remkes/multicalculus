Curl measures the tendency of the vector field to cause local rotation. Using the fluid flow interpretation, if I drop an object in the fluid, it will flow along the integral curves of the vector field. However, as it flows along, it may also start spinning about an axis. Curl measures the tendency of the vector field to cause such a spin. (This is very different from global rotation. The paths of rotation themselves may be circular without actually causing the object itself to spin. Likewise, the paths can be totally straight but still cause local rotation.
