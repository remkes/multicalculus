Definition 1.1.3.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer. Real Euclidean Space or Cartesian Space, written \(\RR^n\text{,}\) is the set of all vectors of length \(n\) with real number entries.
Since there are \(n\) coefficieint in its vectors, the space \(\RR^n\) has dimension \(n\text{.}\)
In this course, I am exclusively interested in \(\RR^2\) (the familiar Cartesian Plane) and in \(\RR^3\) (which I will call Cartesian Three-Space or, more briefly, just Cartesian Soace).
