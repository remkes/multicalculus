Definition 6.3.4.

Let \(I \subset \RR^n\) be an interval and \(f: I \rightarrow \RR\) a function. The Riemann integral of a function is the following limit (if it exists), where the sum is taken over all \(k^n\) subdivision of the interval and \(x_l^*\) is any point in the \(l\)th subinterval, and \(\Delta V\) is the (hyper)volume of the subinterval.
\begin{equation*} \int_{I} f(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n) dV = \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \sum_{l=0}^{k^n} f(x_l^*) \Delta V \end{equation*}
If the limit exists, the function \(f\) is called (Riemann) integrable on the interval \(I\text{.}\)
