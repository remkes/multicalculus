Now think of a function \(f: \RR^2 \rightarrow \RR\text{.}\) To approximate by linear functions, I need to understand linear functions: \(\RR^2 \rightarrow \RR\text{.}\) I look to matrices. Matrices completely describe linear functions. A \(1 \times 1\) matrix is just a number, and the linear function \(\RR \rightarrow \RR\) is just multiplication by that number. But for \(\RR^2 \rightarrow \RR\text{,}\) a \(1 \times 2\) matrix describes the linear function.
