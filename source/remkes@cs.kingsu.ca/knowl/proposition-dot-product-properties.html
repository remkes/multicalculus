Proposition 1.2.12.

Let \(u,v,w\) be vectors in \(\RR^3\) and let \(a\) be a scalar in \(\RR\text{.}\) The following four identities are true for all vectors and scalars.
\begin{align*} u \cdot v \amp = v \cdot u \amp \text{Commutative} \\ u \cdot u \amp = |u|^2 \amp \\ u \cdot (v+w) \amp = u \cdot v + u \cdot w \amp \text{Distributive} \\ u \cdot (av) \amp = (au) \cdot v = a (u \cdot v) \end{align*}
