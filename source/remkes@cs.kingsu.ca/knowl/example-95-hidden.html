Let \(F(x,y) = (x,y)\text{,}\) as in Figure 9.2.6. The curves \(\gamma(t) = (ae^t,be^t)\) where \(a,b \in \RR\) are integral curves, shown in Figure 9.2.7. They have \(x = ae^t\) and \(y = be^t\text{,}\) and I can calculate tangents: \(\gamma^\prime(t) = (ae^t, be^t)\text{.}\) If I compare with the component of the original curve, this is exactly \((x,y)\text{.}\)
