Example 2.1.8.

A spiral in \(\RR^2\) is a parametric curve of the form \(\gamma(t) = (f(t) \cos t, f(t) \sin t)\) where \(f(t)\) is a monotonic continuous function. It is based on the circle, but instead of having a constant radius, the radius is either increasing or decreasing as the curve traces around the circle. The curve \(\gamma(t) = (2e^{\frac{t}{4}} \cos t, 2e^{\frac{t}{4}} \sin t)\) is a logarithmic spiral, as in FigureÂ 2.1.9. For the logarithmic spiral, the parameter \(t\) be any real number: the spiral will spin inwards and outwards without end.
