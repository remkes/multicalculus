Example 6.4.4.

Consider the function \(f(x,y) = \sin (x+y)\) on the interval \(I = [0, \pi] \times \left[0, \frac{\pi}{2} \right]\text{.}\) I do an interated integral calculation with \(x\) inside and \(y\) outside.
\begin{align*} \int_I f(x,y) dx dy \amp = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \int_0^{\pi} \sin (x+y) dx dy\\ \amp = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} (-\cos (x+y)) \bigg|_0^{\pi} dy\\ \amp = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} (\cos y - \cos (y+\pi)) dy = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} 2 \cos y dy\\ \amp = 2 \sin y \bigg|_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} = 2-0 = 2 \end{align*}
