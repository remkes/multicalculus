Example 2.1.2.

The curve \(\gamma(t) = (\cos t, \sin t)\text{,}\) for \(t \in [0, 2\pi]\) traces out a circle, as in Figure 2.1.3. This is an important example: many of the other curves I will use in this course are adaptation of the circle.
Notice that I defined this curve on the domain \([0, 2\pi]\text{.}\) If I extend this domain, the curve just starts to retrace over the circle. It’s good to observe that parametric curves can self-intersect and trace over themselves many times. This is a very different situation from, say, graphs of functions which cannot self-intersect.
