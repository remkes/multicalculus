Definition 2.1.16.

Let \(\gamma(t): [a,b] \rightarrow \RR^3\) be a parametric curve with coordinates \((\gamma_1(t), \gamma_2(t), \gamma_3(t))\text{.}\) A reparametrization of \(\gamma\) is the replacement of the variable \(t\) by a new variable \(u\text{.}\) The two variables are related by a monotonic increasing function \(t = t(u)\) expressing the parameter \(t\) in terms of a new parameter \(u\text{.}\) I replace \(t\) by the function \(t(u)\) to give a parametric curve in terms of \(u\text{:}\) \(\gamma(u) = (\gamma_1(t(u)), \gamma_2(t(u)), \gamma_3(t(u)))\text{.}\)
